## [CHUNK: UNIVERSITY_OVERVIEW] About Aligarh Muslim University (AMU)

**Tags:** general-info, history, location, campus

Aligarh Muslim University (AMU) is one of the oldest premier Central Universities of India, founded by Sir Syed Ahmad Khan. It evolved from a High School (1875) → Mohammadan Anglo Oriental College → Aligarh Muslim University (1920, Act of Parliament).

**Main Campus:** Aligarh, Uttar Pradesh — 130 km southeast of Delhi on the Delhi-Howrah rail route. Latitude 27°54'N, Longitude 78°05'E. Campus is 2 km from Aligarh Railway Station.

**AMU Centres:**
- **Malappuram, Kerala** (est. 2010) — Law and Management
- **Murshidabad, West Bengal** (est. 2010) — Law and Management
- **Kishanganj, Bihar** (est. 2013) — B.Ed.

**Faculties (13):** Agricultural Sciences · Arts · Commerce · Engineering & Technology · International Studies · Law · Life Sciences · Medicine · Management Studies & Research · Science · Social Sciences · Theology · Unani Medicine

**Key Colleges/Institutions:** Women's College · Zakir Husain College of Engineering & Technology · Ajmal Khan Tibbiya College · Jawaharlal Nehru Medical College · Dr. Ziauddin Ahmad Dental College · College of Nursing · Para Medical College · Community College · University Polytechnic · Women's Polytechnic · Centre for Distance & Online Education

## [CHUNK: ADMISSIONS_GENERAL_RULES] General Admission Rules and Important Policies

**Tags:** rules, eligibility, general-policy, important-dates

- Admissions open to **Indian Nationals only** (separate guide for Foreign Nationals/NRI).
- Intake figures are **indicative maximum** — University not bound to admit stated numbers.
- University may **change/modify** eligibility, intake, or processes without prior notice.
- University reserves right **not to run** a course if applicants are fewer than intake.
- **Percentage rounding:** Even a decimal shortfall in marks disqualifies a candidate.
- **Age limit:** Even a single day over/under the limit disqualifies a candidate. Reckoned on **1st July** of admission year.
- Candidates awaiting results may apply but get admission only if they fulfill eligibility at counselling.
- **No candidate** may seek admission to the same Course/Class previously attended.
- Unfair means/impersonation → **disqualification + legal action**.
- Qualifying examination must be recognized by AMU.
- All applications are **online only** at [www.amucontrollerexams.com](http://www.amucontrollerexams.com).
- Separate application required for each course.
- Test fee/processing charges are **non-refundable**.
- Minimum **20% marks in Admission Test** required to be eligible for admission.
- **Closing date for all admissions:** 31.08.2026 (unless separately notified).
- Disputes subject to jurisdiction of **High Court of Judicature at Allahabad, U.P.**

## [CHUNK: INTERNAL_EXTERNAL_DEFINITION] Definition of Internal vs External Candidate

**Tags:** internal, external, candidate-category

**Internal Candidate:** One who has passed the qualifying examination conducted by AMU as a regular student, not earlier than THREE (3) academic years from the date of admission to the course being sought.

**External Candidate:** All others.

- Students who appeared as Private/Distance mode candidates are **not** entitled to Internal category.
- Internal candidates must upload relevant documents with their application; otherwise treated as External.

## [CHUNK: MARKING_SCHEME_OMR] OMR Test Instructions and Negative Marking

**Tags:** test-instructions, OMR, negative-marking, exam-rules

- Medium of all tests: **English** (except language-specific papers).
- OMR Answer Sheet: squares for writing, circles for shading answers.
- **Negative Marking:** Incorrect answer = **–0.25 marks** per question (25% of marks allotted).
- Negative marking **NOT applicable** for: B.Sc. Nursing, Ph.D., and Vocational Courses under Community College.
- Selecting more than one option = treated as incorrect.
- Candidates receive **carbonless duplicate copy** of OMR sheet and Question Booklet after test.
- Question Booklet distributed **15 minutes** after scheduled commencement.
- Candidates must NOT leave seat during test duration except in exigencies.

## [CHUNK: ANSWER_KEY_RESULT] Answer Key Challenge and Result Process

**Tags:** answer-key, challenge, result, counselling

- Answer Key published at [www.amucontrollerexams.com](http://www.amucontrollerexams.com) after MCQ-based tests.
- Candidates can challenge within **48 hours** of Answer Key display, paying **Rs. 100/- per answer** challenged (non-refundable).
- Committee of subject experts reviews challenges; decision is **final**.
- If a question is dropped as incorrect/ambiguous → **full marks to all candidates**.
- No individual intimation on challenge acceptance/rejection.
- **No re-evaluation or re-totalling** requests entertained.

## [CHUNK: PWBD_RULES] Rules for Persons with Benchmark Disabilities (PwBD)

**Tags:** PwBD, disability, reservation, special-category

- **5% seats** reserved over and above approved intake (for most courses).
- For MBBS, BDS, BUMS, B.Sc. Nursing, B.Arch., PG Medicine/Unani Medicine, Ph.D. → reservation **within** approved intake.
- Minimum **40% disability** required (as per Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016).
- Not considered for courses where disability is a hindrance.
- **Test fee:** Rs. 100 only.
- **Admission charges (Tuition Fee):** Rs. 100 only (not applicable for Fellowship holders, Self Finance/NRI/Foreign national seats).
- **Mark relaxation:** 5% in qualifying exam + 5% in Admission/Departmental Test.
- **Age relaxation:** Up to 5 years (except where Statutory/Regulatory Councils prescribe limits).
- Disability Certificate: issued by Medical Board of District/State/UT/Medical Institution of National Importance.
- Admitted PwBD candidates examined by University Medical Board — decision is **final**.
- For MBBS/BDS: certificate only from **NEET(UG)-2026 designated centres**.

## [CHUNK: OUTSTANDING_SPORTSPERSON] Outstanding Sportsperson Category — Rules and Eligibility

**Tags:** sportsperson, SM, sports-category, sports-trial, eligibility

**Eligible Sports:** Athletics, Cricket, Football, Badminton, Basketball, Volleyball, Table Tennis, Wrestling, Weightlifting, Powerlifting, Skating, Hockey, Swimming, Tennis, Horse-Riding (EFI events), Hiking & Mountaineering (recognized institute courses).

**Certificate Requirements:**
- Not more than **2 years old**; from events organized from **01.01.2024** onwards.
- Issued by Federations/Associations approved by IOA/Khelo India/AIU or by University for Zonal/All India Intervarsity.
- No Invitational/Memorial/Open/Prize Money/Ranking/Letter-head certificates accepted.

**Sports Eligibility by Level:**

*Senior Secondary/Diploma:*
- Participation in CBSE/ICSE/KVS/JNV/EMRS/State Board Zonal/Clusters/National Sports Meet; OR State representation in National Championships (IOA/MSYA approved); OR International representation.

*Undergraduate:*
- Top 3 in CBSE/KVS/JNV School Nationals (SGFI) or All India Combined Schools Teams; OR State representation in National Championship; OR International representation.

*Postgraduate:*
- Participated in Zonal or All India Intervarsity (AIU); OR State-level National representation; OR International representation.

**Selection Process:**
1. Must appear in Admission/Departmental Test and secure minimum marks for General Category.
2. Physical Fitness Test (PFT) — qualifier only.
3. Sports Skill Trial (for PFT qualifiers): Fundamental Skills + Game Performance.
4. Additional processing charge: **Rs. 150/-**
5. Only one game/sport per Academic Session.
6. Trial list published 1–2 weeks before trials on AMU website (no postal/email intimation).
7. No TA/DA for travel to Aligarh.
8. **PG Courses:** Must have represented University at least once in Zonal/All India Intervarsity.
9. Maximum **1 seat** per Course/Branch/Specialization under this category.
10. **Closing date:** 31.08.2026.

**Academic relaxation:** Up to **5% in aggregate** percentage for admission under this category.

- **Schools:** SH Sr. Sec. School Boys (650M), STS (55M), AMU City (50M), Sr. Sec. Girls (360F), AMU Girls (120F)

- **Schools:** SH Boys (110M), STS (50M), AMU City (50M), Sr. Sec. Girls (60F)

- **Test:** 26-04-2026, 3:00 PM (Combined with Humanities)

- **Schools:** SH Boys (55M), STS (55M), AMU City (50M), Sr. Sec. Girls (60F), AMU Girls (80F)
