## About Aligarh Muslim University

Aligarh Muslim University (AMU) is a public central university located in Aligarh, Uttar Pradesh, India. Established in 1875 as the Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental College by Sir Syed Ahmad Khan, it was granted university status in 1920 through the Aligarh Muslim University Act. AMU is one of the oldest and most prestigious universities in India and holds the distinction of being a residential university with a unique character.

## History and Founding

Sir Syed Ahmad Khan founded the Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental (MAO) College in 1875 with the vision of providing modern education to Muslims in India. The college was modeled after the University of Oxford and the University of Cambridge. In 1920, the MAO College was elevated to the status of a central university through an act of the Indian legislature, becoming the Aligarh Muslim University.

The establishment of AMU was a landmark event in the history of modern Indian education. Sir Syed believed that modern education was essential for the social and political emancipation of the Muslim community. He was supported by prominent figures including Sir Syed's close associate Sir Sami Ullah Khan and other reformers.

## Key Facts

- **Founded:** 1875 (as MAO College); 1920 (as AMU)
- **Type:** Central University (funded by the Government of India)
- **Chancellor:** The Visitor of the university is the President of India. The Chancellor is the ceremonial head.
- **Vice-Chancellor:** The administrative head of the university.
- **Location:** Aligarh, Uttar Pradesh, India
- **Campus Area:** Approximately 467.6 hectares (1,155 acres)
- **Motto:** "Learn and impart unto others" (علم حاصل کرو اور آگے سکھاؤ)
- **NAAC Accreditation:** A++ Grade
- **NIRF Ranking:** Consistently ranked among the top universities in India

## Campus

The AMU campus is one of the largest university campuses in Asia, spread over approximately 467.6 hectares. The campus is a self-contained township with its own infrastructure, including residential areas, markets, hospitals, schools, and places of worship.

Key landmarks on campus include:
- **Strachey Hall:** The main administrative building
- **Victoria Gate:** The iconic entrance to the university
- **Maulana Azad Library:** One of the largest university libraries in Asia
- **Sir Syed Hall:** A historic building used for functions and gatherings
- **Kennedy House:** Named after a former principal
- **University Mosque:** A beautiful mosque within the campus

## Vision and Mission

AMU's vision is to promote education and learning while maintaining its unique character as a minority institution. The university aims to:
- Provide world-class education across diverse disciplines
- Foster research and innovation
- Promote national integration and communal harmony
- Preserve the rich cultural heritage of India
- Serve as a centre of excellence in higher education

## Administration

The university is administered through a well-defined governance structure:
- **The Visitor:** The President of India
- **The Chancellor:** Ceremonial head of the university
- **The Vice-Chancellor:** The principal executive and academic officer
- **The Executive Council:** The principal executive body
- **The Academic Council:** The principal academic body
- **The Court:** The supreme governing body
