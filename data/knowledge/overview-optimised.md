## CHUNK 2 — History & Founding

**Topic:** History of AMU | Founding | Sir Syed Ahmad Khan

- AMU traces its origins to 1875 when **Sir Syed Ahmad Khan** founded the **Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental (MAO) College** in Aligarh.
- The college was modeled after the **University of Oxford** and the **University of Cambridge**, combining modern Western education with Islamic values.
- Sir Syed's motivation was the **social and political emancipation of the Muslim community** through modern education.
- Key supporters included **Sir Sami Ullah Khan** and other reformers of the era.
- In **1920**, the Indian legislature elevated MAO College to a full **Central University** through the **Aligarh Muslim University Act**, establishing AMU as an institution.
- AMU is one of the **oldest and most prestigious universities** in India.
- It holds the distinction of being a **residential university with a unique minority character**.

**Key Timeline:**
- 1875 — MAO College founded
- 1920 — AMU Act passed; university status granted
- Present — NAAC A+ accredited; Top 10 NIRF ranked

## CHUNK 3 — Campus & Infrastructure

**Topic:** AMU Campus, Landmarks, Facilities

- The AMU campus is one of the **largest university campuses in Asia**, spread over ~467.6 hectares (1,155 acres).
- The campus functions as a **self-contained township** with residential areas, markets, hospitals, schools, and places of worship.
- AMU has **20 Halls of Residence** accommodating students on campus.

## CHUNK 7 — Vision, Mission & Character

**Topic:** AMU vision, mission, minority institution, goals

AMU's vision is to promote education and learning while maintaining its unique character as a **minority institution** under the Indian Constitution. Key goals include:

- Providing world-class education across diverse disciplines
- Fostering research and innovation
- Promoting national integration and communal harmony
- Preserving the rich cultural heritage of India
- Serving as a centre of excellence in higher education

AMU draws students from **all corners of India** as well as international students, particularly from **Africa, West Asia, and Southeast Asia**, reflecting its global character.
