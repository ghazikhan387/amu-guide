## Hostel System at AMU

Aligarh Muslim University is a residential university, and hostel life is an integral part of the AMU experience. The university has a well-established hostel system with separate hostels for male and female students.

## Men's Hostels (Halls of Residence)

AMU has several historic halls of residence for male students:

1. **Sir Syed Hall (North & South)** — One of the oldest and most prestigious hostels, named after the university's founder. Known for its grand architecture and traditions.

2. **Aftab Hall** — A large hall accommodating undergraduate and postgraduate students.

3. **Sir Syed Hall (South)** — Extension of the original Sir Syed Hall.

4. **Sulaiman Hall** — Named after Justice Sir Shah Sulaiman, one of the first Indian Chief Justices.

5. **Mumtaz Hall** — A hall for postgraduate students.

6. **M.M. Hall (Muhammad Habib Hall)** — A residential hall for senior students.

7. **Allama Iqbal Hall** — Named after the great poet-philosopher Allama Muhammad Iqbal.

8. **Ross Masood Hall** — Named after Sir Ross Masood, a grandson of Sir Syed Ahmad Khan.

9. **Hadi Hasan Hall** — Named after a distinguished professor of the university.

10. **S.T.S. Hall (Sir Syed Training School)** — Primarily for students of the University Polytechnic.

11. **Nadeem Tarin Hall** — A newer hall of residence.

12. **Hamid Hall** — Named after a distinguished member of the university community.

## Women's Hostels

1. **Abdullah Hall** — The main women's hall of residence.
2. **Indira Subhash Hall** — For undergraduate women students.
3. **Non-Resident Women Students' Centre** — Facilities for non-resident women students.

## Hostel Facilities

Each hostel typically provides:
- **Accommodation:** Shared rooms (double or triple occupancy for most halls)
- **Common Rooms:** For socialising and recreation, often equipped with TVs, newspapers, and indoor games
- **Dining Hall (Mess):** Each hall has its own mess serving breakfast, lunch, and dinner. Students pay monthly mess fees.
- **Reading Room / Library:** A quiet space for study
- **Wi-Fi:** Internet connectivity available in most hostels
- **Laundry:** Laundry services or facilities
- **Sports Facilities:** Some halls have their own cricket pitches, badminton courts, and other sports areas
- **Canteen / Tea Stall:** Small canteens or tea stalls within or near the hostel
- **Security:** 24/7 security and entry/exit monitoring

## Hostel Fees

Hostel fees at AMU are highly subsidised and among the lowest of any major university in India:
- **Room Rent:** Approximately ₹500–₹2,000 per semester (varies by hall)
- **Mess Fees:** Approximately ₹3,000–₹5,000 per month (varies by mess)
- **Electricity Charges:** Included or nominal additional charges
- **Security Deposit:** Refundable deposit at the time of admission

*Exact fees may vary each year. Check the official AMU website for current rates.*

## Hostel Rules and Regulations

- All resident students must abide by the hostel rules set by the Provost/Warden.
- Entry and exit times are regulated, especially for women's hostels.
- Ragging is strictly prohibited and punishable.
- Guests must be registered at the hostel gate.
- Students are expected to maintain discipline and cleanliness.
- Each hall is managed by a Provost (for men's halls) or Warden (for women's halls) who is a senior faculty member.

## How to Get Hostel Accommodation

- Hostel allotment is done at the time of admission.
- Priority is given to outstation students.
- Students apply for hostel accommodation along with their admission application.
- Allotment is subject to availability.
- Local students may apply for day-scholar hostel access.
